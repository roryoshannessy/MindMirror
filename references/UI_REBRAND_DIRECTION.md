# MindMirror UI Rebrand Direction

Last updated: June 1, 2026

Purpose: translate competitor research into concrete UI direction for the web app.

## Core Direction

MindMirror should feel:

- minimal, premium, black-and-white first
- calm enough to use when tired
- voice-first, with text as the safe fallback
- focused on pattern reveal, not generic wellness content
- sparse, confident, and low-friction

The product moment:

```text
Say the thought.
See the loop.
Take one next step.
```

## What To Borrow From Stoic

- one question or action per screen
- bold confident type
- lots of negative space
- anchored primary CTA
- simple option pills
- daily home card
- subtle progress indicators
- completion moments after reflection
- voice capture sheet with transcript review
- a minimal brand character used sparingly

Do not copy:

- streak pressure as the main motivation
- many wellness content sections
- mentor/persona complexity
- rich text/drawing tools
- generic inspiration/library tabs

## What To Borrow From Rosebud

- voice journaling is a core feature, not a nice-to-have
- weekly insights validate the pattern-memory direction
- users understand daily progress and tracking
- privacy/trust needs to be visible
- pattern reporting is a direct competitor promise

Do not copy:

- gradient-heavy app-store visual style
- broad "heal/grow" positioning
- generic AI companion language

## What To Avoid From Day One

Day One is a trusted life archive. MindMirror is not trying to become a scrapbook.

Avoid:

- media-first archive UI
- timeline-heavy browsing as the primary experience
- desktop-document density
- "your journal for life" positioning

Learn:

- import/export and ownership matter later
- journal history can become a source for pattern analysis

## MindMirror Web App Shape

Because MindMirror is web-based, remove as much friction as possible.

Default `/account` experience:

1. Daily card: "What keeps coming back today?"
2. Capture: voice or text.
3. Mirror reveal: possible loop, cost, evidence, question, next action.
4. Evidence ladder: one thought, three thoughts, ten thoughts.
5. Pattern memory: only becomes prominent after enough entries.

No forced app onboarding before first use.

## Evidence Ladder

Use truthful unlocks, not manipulative gamification:

- 1 reflection: First mirror unlocked.
- 3 reflections: Early signals unlocked.
- 10 reflections: Pattern map unlocked.

Language:

```text
Build enough evidence to unlock your pattern map.
```

This explains why MindMirror gets better over time and gives users a reason to upload/import older journals later.

## Mascot Direction

Explore a minimal brand device:

- a simple brain holding a round mirror
- black/white line-art or silhouette
- subtle motion only
- no face or cute expressions
- used for onboarding, empty states, and "analysing" transitions

The mascot should not replace the UI. It should mark the moment where the thought becomes a mirror.

## Key Rule

Capture first. Categorize later. Analyse after saving.
